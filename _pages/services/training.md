    <h2>Open-Source Modelling & Simulation Training</h2>

    <p>
      StrataFlux Energy provides practical, workflow-based technical training in reservoir simulation, geochemical modelling, reactive transport analysis, and computational workflows for students, researchers, engineers, and professional teams.
    </p>

    <p>
      Our training is designed to help participants move beyond software operation and develop a deeper understanding of the physics, assumptions, numerical workflows, and technical reasoning behind subsurface modelling studies.
    </p>

    <div class="highlight-box">
      <p>
        <strong>Training philosophy:</strong> We focus on applied learning, real modelling workflows, hands-on exercises, and practical interpretation, helping participants build confidence in both the tools and the science behind the simulations.
      </p>
    </div>

    <hr>

    <h3>Training Areas</h3>

    <ul>
      <li>Reservoir simulation fundamentals and workflow development</li>
      <li>Black oil and compositional reservoir modelling concepts</li>
      <li>CO₂ storage modelling and plume migration analysis</li>
      <li>Underground hydrogen storage modelling and cyclic storage workflows</li>
      <li>Reactive transport and geochemical modelling</li>
      <li>PHREEQC-based geochemical analysis</li>
      <li>PFLOTRAN-based reactive transport modelling</li>
      <li>MRST-based reservoir simulation workflows</li>
      <li>Python for post-processing, plotting, automation, and sensitivity analysis</li>
      <li>Uncertainty evaluation and simulation result interpretation</li>
    </ul>

    <hr>

    <h3>Who This Training Is For</h3>

    <ul>
      <li>Students working on simulation-based BSc, MSc, or PhD projects</li>
      <li>Researchers developing subsurface energy, geochemical, or reactive transport models</li>
      <li>Reservoir engineers seeking practical exposure to open-source modelling tools</li>
      <li>Energy professionals interested in CO₂ storage, hydrogen storage, or reservoir forecasting workflows</li>
      <li>University groups seeking applied modelling workshops</li>
      <li>Organizations looking to build internal technical capacity in subsurface simulation</li>
    </ul>

    <hr>

    <h3>Training Formats</h3>

    <ul>
      <li>One-to-one technical mentoring sessions</li>
      <li>Small-group online workshops</li>
      <li>Custom training for research groups and academic teams</li>
      <li>Industry-focused short courses for technical staff</li>
      <li>Project-based training built around specific modelling workflows</li>
      <li>Hands-on coding, simulation setup, troubleshooting, and result interpretation sessions</li>
    </ul>

    <hr>

    <h3>Practical Skills Participants Develop</h3>

    <ul>
      <li>How to design a clear modelling workflow from research or engineering objectives</li>
      <li>How to define model assumptions, input parameters, boundary conditions, and scenarios</li>
      <li>How to troubleshoot simulation errors and interpret numerical outputs</li>
      <li>How to extract, visualize, and explain simulation results professionally</li>
      <li>How to automate repetitive modelling tasks using Python</li>
      <li>How to carry out sensitivity analysis and compare simulation scenarios</li>
      <li>How to connect modelling results to practical subsurface decisions</li>
    </ul>

    <hr>

    <h3>Computational & AI-Assisted Learning Workflows</h3>

    <p>
      We integrate modern computational workflows into our training, including scripting, automation, structured data handling, visualization, and AI-assisted support for coding productivity, workflow organization, and simulation result interpretation.
    </p>

    <p>
      The aim is not to replace technical understanding, but to help participants use modern tools responsibly to improve efficiency, reduce repetitive work, and strengthen their ability to analyse complex subsurface modelling problems.
    </p>

    <hr>

    <h3>What Makes Our Training Different</h3>

    <ul>
      <li>Training is grounded in real subsurface modelling problems, not generic software demonstrations.</li>
      <li>Sessions combine theory, workflow design, simulation practice, and result interpretation.</li>
      <li>Participants learn the reasoning behind modelling choices, not only which buttons or commands to use.</li>
      <li>Training can be tailored to CO₂ storage, hydrogen storage, reservoir forecasting, reactive transport, or geochemical modelling topics.</li>
      <li>Support can be adapted for beginners, intermediate users, or advanced research workflows.</li>
    </ul>
